About Me

The accounting life was just not for me. After 4 years in the accounting field, I realized that it was not something I was passionate about. I realized that the aspect of accounting that I like is Excel, creating formulas in Excel, and balancing/investigating variances. I started to take a few free online coding courses and realized that what I like about accounting is transferable to coding. After a few months of the free online coding course, it reminded me of why I became a math major. I love building functions, using formulas, and problem-solving. That is when I decided I wanted to become a software engineer. Being able to analyze data, collaborating with other teams and teammates, and knowing how to prioritize my work and meet deadlines make me a better software engineer.

With my accounting and mathematics background, I love to investigate, find bugs, and bring them to resolution. Being an engineer, I think it is really fun to be able to apply my degree and work background while creating a product.

To pursue a career change, I enrolled in AppAcademyâ€™s coding bootcamp where I learned Python, React, Redux, JavaScript, PostgreSQL, HTML, CSS, and many other technologies.

In the next 5 years, I would like to learn new frameworks and languages, be able to apply the knowledge and skills I gained from App Academy, and work for a company that values career growth.

While I am not working or coding, I like to play with my shiba inu, go climbing, and relax in the backyard when the weather is nice.
